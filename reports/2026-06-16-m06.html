2026-06-16 · M06 — 3D simple-cubic Ising
I ran the simple-cubic 3D Ising model on an L=12 lattice across a temperature window straddling the transition and tracked the magnetic susceptibility χ(T). Three dimensions has no exact solution, so the target is the Monte-Carlo benchmark T_c ≈ 4.5115. The χ peak sits at T_c(L) = 4.504. Wall time on the CPU: 28s.
✓ χ-peak T_c(L) = 4.504 vs MC benchmark 4.5115 (rel. err 0.2%), and the specific-heat peak independently gives T_c(L) = 4.420. The transition is in the right place — the lab reproduces the 3D benchmark, calibrating Phase 2. (Small-L finite-size effects push the pseudo-critical peak slightly above the infinite-volume T_c; an L-extrapolation would sharpen the number — see BACKLOG.)
