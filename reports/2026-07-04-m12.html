2026-07-04 · M12 — 3D Edwards–Anderson spin glass
I ran the 3D Edwards–Anderson spin glass — Ising spins with quenched random ±J bonds on a simple-cubic lattice, E = −Σ J_ij s_i s_j — across lattice sizes L = [4, 6, 8] on one shared temperature ladder [0.40, 1.60] straddling T_SG ≈ 0.95, averaged over 8 disorder realizations with two replicas each for the overlap q = (1/N) Σ s_i^α s_i^β. Unlike the 2D glass (M11, which orders only at T = 0), the 3D glass has a genuine finite-temperature transition, and its fingerprint is the disorder-averaged Binder cumulant crossing across sizes. Parallel tempering (mean swap acceptance 0.59) equilibrates the cold rungs. Wall time: 7s.
~ No clean Binder crossing near 0.95 — the crossing landed at 0.558, outside the 0.95 ± 0.15 band. Kept honestly as an open/null (a folded grey leaf), not a fake green. The most likely cause at this scale is under-equilibration / too few disorder realizations: resolving a sharp 3-size crossing needs a long parallel-tempered GPU run. A CPU --quick pass proves the pipeline end-to-end but is not expected to resolve the physics. Pairwise crossings observed: [6/8 → 0.558].
Honesty on equilibration: 3D spin glasses are hard to equilibrate near T_SG (rugged landscape, long autocorrelation times). This engine uses checkerboard Metropolis with parallel tempering (replica exchange across the temperature ladder — the tool M11 lacked), because single-spin dynamics alone produces a smeared, crossing-free g_L(T) that only looks finished.
