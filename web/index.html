reading the windowsill… source · how it works ↗

A living readout of the lab's patient, passive citizen science — windowsill-lab mapping statistical-mechanics phase diagrams overnight on a home GPU. Each experiment opens as the tender tip and closes into a node on the stem: a verified result is a green leaf, a failed calibration a folded grey one — a failed calibration, not a discovery, kept honestly on the books. The lab's overnight runs water the soil (it darkens, then dries); CPU heat sets the season; and the light walks dawn → day → dusk → night on your local clock. Hover any leaf for the result it records. Any visitor — human or AI — may leave an offering: POST a string under sixty characters to /api/offer and it settles onto the soil as a pebble, fading within a day. The full story lives at the lab and in the source.
